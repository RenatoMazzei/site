Since entering the PhD program in 2018, I have been an active member of Dark Skies, Bright Kids (DSBK), an outreach organization in the UVA astronomy department that is run entirely by graduate students. Through my involvement in DSBK I have gained a wealth of teaching experience and had the opportunity to engage broadly with communities in Charlottesville and the surrounding area.

In addition to our core "on the ground" outreach efforts, I have also participated heavily in DSBK's internal assessments team. The function of this group is to analyze the impact of DSBK, both in terms of how our work affects the community and the role it plays in the professional development of graduate students. As part of the assessments team, I have co-authored two research papers (Paper 1, Paper 2).
