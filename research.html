I am interested in understanding the role of magnetic fields in star formation by comparing synthetic polarization observations with real data obtained from observatories such as ALMA, SOFIA, and BLAST. In my work I perform 3D magnetohydrodynamic simulations and set up 3D analytic models to be used as input for radiative transfer calculations with codes such as POLARIS and RADMC3D.

For my PhD thesis I undertook three main projects, each aimed at a different scale of the star formation process:

Thesis Project 1 - The Zeeman Effect in Protoplanetary Disks (published in ApJ)

Thesis Project 2 - The HRO Technique in Molecular Clouds (published in MNRAS)

Thesis Project 3 - The Zeeman Effect in Protostellar Envelopes (paper submitted to MNRAS)

In addition to my main thesis work, I have also collaborated on other projects with members of the BLAST theory group, done research on some of the important effects of science outreach, and been the principal investigator of a successeful ALMA observing proposal. A full list of my works can be found in my CV.
